Van Gog

On 1 May 1888, Van Gogh signed a lease for four rooms at 2 Place Lamartine, Arles, which he later painted in The Yellow House(Van Gog (1888)).[126] The rooms cost 15 francs per month, unfurnished; they had been uninhabited for months.[127] Because the Yellow House had to be furnished before he could fully move in, Van Gogh moved from the Hôtel Carrel to the Café de la Gare on 7 May 1888.[128] He had befriended the Yellow House's proprietors, Joseph and Marie Ginoux, and was able to use it as a studio.[129] Van Gogh wanted a gallery to display his work and started a series of paintings that eventually included Van Gog(1888)(Van Gogh's Chair (1888)), Bedroom in Arles (1888), The Night Café (1888), Café Terrace at Night(September 1888)(Van Gog (September 1888)) , Starry Night Over the Rhone (1888), and Still Life: Vase with Twelve Sunflowers (1888), all intended for the decoration for the Yellow House.
